Hourglass — meteor shower + day/night mockup

Top chamber: a meteor shower falls, each meteor landing in the sand with a splash of flung grains + an impact flash. Bottom chamber: an independent sky cycle — the sun arcs down, sets through dusk, and night falls to near-black as stars emerge. Canvas drives the interior; the SVG glass shell sits on top.

What's happening: the top is a meteor emitter — each meteor falls with a glowing trail, hits the sand surface (the funnel line), throws a burst of sand grains and a quick flash, then the grains arc back down. The bottom runs a ~22s sky clock: day → the sun arcs down and sets → dusk colours → night fades to near-black with stars twinkling in → loops. The neck keeps a thin sand stream running between them. In production this is the v4 canvas engine with these two emitters added; the glass shell + frame are the SVG we locked.

Can't pre-render here — if a layer's misaligned, the sun clips oddly, or the meteors land in the wrong place, tell me and I'll nudge coordinates.
